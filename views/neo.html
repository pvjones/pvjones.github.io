Potentially Hazardous
Asteroids (PHAs)

NASA designates objects as PHAs when they will pass within 0.05 AU (roughly 7,480,000 km or 4,650,000 mi) and are larger than 150 m in diameter.

There are currently 1767 known PHAs.

This does not mean a PHA will impact the Earth. It only means there is a slight possibility for such a threat. By monitoring PHAs and updating their data NASA can better predict the close-approach statistics and thus their Earth-impact threat.
